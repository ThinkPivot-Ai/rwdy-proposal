We went through the data shared by the team, ran a live audit of the website, tested brand search across Google, ChatGPT, and Perplexity, and reviewed the app store listings. This document is the output — structured as: here's where you are, here's what the numbers mean, here's what's leaking, how to fix it, and what that gets you to.

The short version: RWDY has real product-market fit. Strong AOV, above-average prepaid ratio, 3% return rate, and a founder with 22M+ followers. None of that is the constraint. What's constraining growth is a cluster of execution failures — most fixable in weeks, without additional ad spend. The April–July window is the right time to fix before the October season opens.

RTO — 25% of orders going out and coming back

What's happening

288 orders every month leave the warehouse, travel to the customer, fail to deliver, and return. The customer never gets the product. RWDY pays shipping twice and earns nothing.

Why this is happening

Rohit orders a ₹2,130 RWDY hoodie at 11pm on COD after seeing Vijay's Instagram story. Wednesday the delivery guy calls — Rohit doesn't recognise the number, misses it. Thursday: second attempt, Rohit is at work. Friday: package returns to the warehouse.

  • Impulsive COD orders placed late at night — no follow-up confirmation
  • Zero post-order communication — customer forgets the order exists
  • No tracking updates — customer doesn't know when to expect delivery
  • No pre-delivery heads-up message before arrival

Unit economics of this leak

  • Forward shipping per order: ₹60
  • Return shipping per order: ₹80
  • Repackaging / restocking: ₹20
  • Blocked revenue (order value): ₹2,130
  • Total cost per RTO order: ₹2,290

At 288 RTO orders/month: ₹6.6L in total monthly leakage. Fixing to 11% recovers 161 orders. Revenue recovered: ₹3.43L. Logistics saved: ₹0.26L. Total recovery: ₹3.7L/month.

Why 161 and not 288? Some RTO is irreducible — wrong addresses, genuine courier failure. 11% is the realistic floor for a well-run D2C brand. The 161 orders are the gap between current and benchmark.

The fix — zero new ad spend

✓ Implementation — 2–3 days, WhatsApp API tool

1. WhatsApp COD confirmation within 30 mins of order — address verification, non-reply triggers pre-dispatch call.
2. Automated tracking update at every courier status change.
3. Pre-delivery address confirmation 24 hours before arrival.

Tool: Interakt or Wati — ₹5,000–8,000/month flat. No dev work required.

Q3 — July to September 2026
National Expansion Phase · Target: ₹52L–₹65L/month
What this quarter is about

Foundation is fixed. Fixes are compounding. Now you expand geographically and restructure paid media to deliver the ROAS it should have been delivering all along. The lull before October gives time to build the campaigns, test the creatives, and seed influencers in new markets — so when October hits, it hits at full capacity.

Key Initiatives
  • Meta campaign restructure — 3-tier funnel: TOF (fans of Telugu cinema + streetwear interest in Mumbai, Delhi, Pune), MOF (retargeting product viewers + cart abandoners), BOF (past purchaser upsell). Move ROAS from 1.99× to 3.5–4.5×
  • North & West India expansion — Dedicated campaign sets for Mumbai, Delhi, Pune. Hinglish copy. Mumbai/Delhi street culture references — not Telugu touchpoints. Micro-influencer seeding in Hindi-belt streetwear community
  • Marketplace activation — List curated catalog on Myntra. Select Culture Line on Ajio Luxe for premium perception. Start building Tier 2/3 reach without D2C logistics burden
  • Women's capsule development — Design and sample a 6–8 piece women's streetwear line. Launch in Q4
  • Drop cadence to 2/month — With hype sequence template built, scale to 2 structured drops per month. Each with pre-launch, waitlist, coordinated push
  • October campaign pre-build — Campaigns, creatives, influencer deals, and inventory plans all locked by end of September
Revenue drivers
Fixed funnel (₹48L baseline) + Meta restructure (+₹6–8L) + North India campaigns (+₹5–8L) + Myntra activation (+₹3–5L) + second hype drop per month (+₹4L) = ₹65–70L by September
Q4 — October to December 2026
Peak Season Push · Target: ₹75L–₹1Cr+/month
What this quarter is about

October–December is RWDY's historically best period. With a fixed funnel, national campaigns, a retention engine firing, and 2 structured drops per month — this is when everything compounds. November is the month to hit ₹1Cr for the first time. The entire year builds to this.

Key Initiatives
  • Full national Meta + Google push — Scale spend 30–40% on proven campaigns. Both brand and non-brand. All geographies firing. Performance-Max for top SKUs
  • Signature October drop — The year's biggest drop. Pre-launch starts mid-September. Vijay-fronted campaign. Culture collab (Indian artisan / musician) for earned media. Target 60%+ sell-through in first 48 hours
  • Women's capsule launch — Timed to October-November. Separate campaign set targeting female streetwear buyers in metros. Opens a new acquisition channel
  • Rowdy Coins reactivation — Loyalty programme push to app base. Redeem coins before year end. Drives repeat purchase in the highest-revenue months
  • Pop-up / experiential — Physical activation in Mumbai or Bengaluru for brand heat and press. Modelled on the Hyderabad event that drove 91% sales spike
  • Influencer week — Coordinated seeding to 20–30 micro-influencers across North + South simultaneously. All posting the same week as the October drop
Revenue target
₹75L in October → ₹1Cr+ in November (first time) → ₹90–95L in December (slight post-peak dip). Q4 total: ₹2.65–₹2.95Cr
